    Exercise Take advantage of monocular cues to implement a 2D sketch to trick the eye into perceiving a 3D scene. Parallax Illusion # The parallax illusion is a monocular cue that occurs when an observer’s viewpoint changes. It is the apparent shift in the relative positions of objects caused by the motion of the observer. When the observer moves, the position of nearby objects changes more than that of distant objects, creating a relative motion between the objects in the foreground and the background.
